## The Challenge

The client had a well-funded product and strong brand recognition among investors, but almost no organic search presence. Their previous agency had produced blog content without addressing fundamental technical issues — crawl budget waste, duplicate meta, and a JavaScript-heavy architecture that Google struggled to index.

Monthly organic sessions when we started: **8,300**. Their roadmap needed that to be 30,000+ within a year.

## Our Approach

### 1. Technical Foundation First

Before touching content, we ran a full crawl audit using Screaming Frog and cross-referenced it with Google Search Console data. Key findings:

- 34% of pages were not indexed due to `noindex` tags left from development
- JavaScript-rendered content was going unindexed — Google's crawler saw blank pages
- Duplicate title tags across 120+ pages were cannibalising keyword authority
- Core Web Vitals: LCP was 5.8s on mobile, INP was 720ms

We resolved all critical issues within the first six weeks, working directly with their engineering team to implement server-side rendering for key landing pages.

### 2. Keyword Architecture Rebuild

We mapped every service page, feature page, and blog post against a unified keyword architecture — eliminating internal cannibalisation and ensuring each page owned a clear search intent cluster.

### 3. Content Sprint

With the technical foundation solid, we ran a 12-week content sprint targeting 45 high-intent keywords across three clusters: personal finance tools, business banking, and compliance. Each piece was written with E-E-A-T signals built in — expert quotes, original data, and clear authorship.

### 4. Core Web Vitals

We worked with the engineering team to:
- Implement `next/image` with proper sizing and lazy loading
- Move to edge-cached static pages for all marketing content
- Replace a heavy third-party chat widget with a lightweight alternative

Lighthouse performance score moved from 61 to **98**.

## Results

Eight months after engagement start, the numbers spoke clearly:

The client exceeded their 30,000 organic sessions target two months ahead of schedule.
