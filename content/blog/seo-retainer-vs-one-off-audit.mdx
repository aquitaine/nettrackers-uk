Clients ask us this question every week: *should I commission a one-off SEO audit, or sign a monthly retainer?* The honest answer depends on where you are and what you need right now.

## What a one-off audit delivers

A technical or content SEO audit is a **diagnostic document**. It tells you:
- What is broken or misconfigured on your site today
- Which issues have the highest ranking impact
- Exactly what your developers need to fix

Audits are ideal when:
- You have just launched or redesigned your site
- Rankings have dropped unexpectedly and you need to understand why
- You want a second opinion before hiring an agency
- You have budget for a single engagement, not an ongoing commitment

The limitation: an audit is a snapshot in time. Google's algorithm updates, competitors move, and your site's content changes. An audit from 18 months ago is not a reliable guide to today's priorities.

## What a monthly retainer delivers

A retainer is **continuous SEO management** — monitoring, strategy, implementation support, and reporting as an ongoing service. It includes:
- Monthly crawls and CWV checks to catch regressions
- Content strategy and brief creation
- Outreach and link acquisition
- Regular reporting tied to your business KPIs

Retainers are right when:
- You want to grow organic traffic month-on-month
- You are in a competitive vertical where rivals are actively investing in SEO
- You need someone to own the SEO roadmap, not just produce a to-do list

## A practical decision framework

| Situation | Recommended |
|---|---|
| Pre-launch or post-redesign | One-off technical audit |
| Unexplained ranking drop | One-off audit |
| Steady site, want steady growth | Monthly retainer |
| Competitive niche, long-term ambition | Retainer from the start |
| Limited budget, DIY implementation | Audit + quarterly check-ins |

## Why not both?

Many clients start with an audit — so they understand the baseline — and then move into a retainer once the critical fixes are live. That sequencing gives us a shared foundation and means retainer hours are spent on growth, not firefighting.
