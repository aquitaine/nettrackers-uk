Every London startup founder we speak to has a version of the same regret: *"We wish we had started SEO earlier."* The reason is simple — organic search compounds, and compounding takes time.

## SEO is not a tap you can turn on

Paid search (Google Ads, Meta) delivers traffic the moment you start spending. SEO does not. A new domain typically takes **six to twelve months** to earn meaningful rankings, even for a well-executed strategy. That lag is structural — Google needs to see crawl history, click data, and link acquisition before it trusts a site with high-visibility positions.

If you launch paid campaigns on day one but ignore SEO, you are funding Google's ad revenue instead of building an asset. Every month you delay is a month of compounding you will never recover.

## The cost of retrofitting

Most SEO mistakes are architectural. They are baked into CMS choice, URL structure, internal linking, and page template design. Fixing them after launch typically means:
- Engineering time to restructure URLs (with redirect mapping)
- CMS migrations that introduce new crawl issues
- Rewriting content that was never optimised
- Months waiting for Google to re-crawl and re-rank

Getting it right on day one costs a fraction of the retrofit.

## What "from day one" actually means

You do not need a six-figure SEO agency from your first week. You need:

1. **A domain and hosting strategy** — avoid subdomains for core content; use a fast, crawlable stack (Next.js is excellent for this)
2. **Clean URL architecture** — logical, keyword-informed URL paths that will not need restructuring
3. **Baseline on-page hygiene** — unique titles, meta descriptions, `<h1>` per page, canonical tags
4. **A content plan** — even three to five blog posts per quarter builds topical authority faster than zero
5. **Google Search Console configured** — so you have data from day one, not from whenever you get round to it

## The London competitive landscape

London is one of the most competitive markets in Europe for almost every B2B category — professional services, fintech, legal, recruitment, SaaS. Your competitors have been investing in SEO for years. The good news: most of them have done it badly. A technically excellent site with well-researched content can overtake a domain-authority-heavy competitor if the execution is right.

The bad news: if you wait two years, you are not catching up to where they are today. You are catching up to where they will be in two years.

## Where to start

If you are pre-launch or in your first six months:
- Get a technical SEO review of your stack before launch
- Commission a keyword strategy for your core product and content pillars
- Set up GSC and GA4 immediately

If you are post-launch with no SEO investment yet:
- Start with a technical audit to understand what you are working with
- Fix critical issues, then build content from there
